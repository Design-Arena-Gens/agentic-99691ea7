2. Third-Party Carbon Verification Report (ISO 14064-3 compliant)

Executive Summary

Fabtech International Ltd. engaged an independent verifier to perform a limited assurance engagement over its 2024 greenhouse gas inventory in accordance with ISO 14064-3, against the criteria of ISO 14064-1 and the WRI/WBCSD GHG Protocol. Reported Scopes 1 and 2 market-based emissions total 13,095 tCO₂e. The verifier concludes, based on the procedures performed and evidence obtained, that nothing has come to our attention that causes us to believe the GHG statement is not prepared, in all material respects, in accordance with the stated criteria.

1.0 Objective and Criteria

  • Objective: Provide limited level assurance over 2024 Scopes 1 and 2, and qualitative review of selected Scope 3 categories.
  • Criteria: ISO 14064-1:2018, GHG Protocol Corporate Standard, location- and market-based methodologies.

2.0 Scope and Boundaries

Organizational boundary: operational control over JAFZA facilities in Dubai, UAE. Reporting period: 1 Jan 2024 to 31 Dec 2024. Gases: CO₂, CH₄, N₂O (reported in CO₂e). Scopes: 1 (stationary combustion, fleet) and 2 (purchased electricity); selected Scope 3 (purchased steel, upstream logistics, business travel) reviewed for completeness but not assured.

3.0 Methodology

  • Risk-based approach per ISO 14064-3, focusing on material sources and data flows.
  • Sampling of activity data (utility bills, diesel invoices, meter logs); recalculation checks; arithmetic accuracy.
  • Emission factors: UAE market-based residual mix, DEFRA/IEA for fuels; GWP AR6 values.

4.0 Materiality and Uncertainty

Materiality threshold set at 5% of total Scopes 1+2. Estimated overall uncertainty is 3.1%, driven by electricity factor variability and diesel stock reconcilation.

5.0 Findings

SourceActivityData QualitytCO₂eObservation
Scope 1Diesel combustionHigh8,940Robust invoices; stock variance <2%
Scope 2Purchased electricity (market-based)High4,155Supplier emission factor evidence retained
Scope 3Purchased steelMediumn/aEcoinvent proxy; recommend supplier-specific EPDs
Scope 3Upstream transportMediumn/aDistance-based model; improve carrier data capture

6.0 Verification Statement

Based on the procedures performed and evidence obtained, nothing has come to our attention to indicate that Fabtech’s 2024 GHG assertion of 13,095 tCO₂e (Scopes 1 and 2 market-based) is materially misstated. This conclusion is provided for the intended use by Fabtech management and stakeholders and may not be suitable for other purposes. Level of assurance: limited. Standard: ISO 14064-3.

7.0 Recommendations

  • Expand supplier-specific emission factors for steel purchases through EPD collection.
  • Automate meter data capture and reconciliation within an energy management system.
  • Enhance Scope 3 coverage to include downstream transport and capital goods.

3. Biodiversity Impact Assessment and Management Plan

1.0 Purpose

Identify and manage biodiversity risks and opportunities associated with Fabtech’s industrial activities in JAFZA, ensuring alignment with ISO 14001:2015 and EcoVadis best practices.

2.0 Scope

All lands leased in JAFZA, adjacent right-of-way, and logistics routes to nearby ports. Focus on terrestrial habitats within the industrial free zone and coastal proximity to the Arabian Gulf.

3.0 Policy Statement

Fabtech commits to avoiding biodiversity loss, minimizing habitat disturbance, preventing invasive species spread, and protecting sensitive receptors through operational controls and supplier engagement.

4.0 Responsibilities

  • Board ESG Committee: oversight and target approval.
  • HSE Manager: plan ownership, monitoring, and reporting.
  • Project Managers: method statements and site-specific controls.
  • Logistics: routing and spill prevention.

4. Product Lifecycle and Circular Economy Strategy

1.0 Purpose

Embed circularity into design, procurement, manufacturing, logistics, and end-of-life for steel structures and industrial equipment manufactured by Fabtech.

2.0 Scope

Product design, material selection (steel grades), packaging, service and refurbishment, and reverse logistics with customers and suppliers.

3.0 Strategy and Policy

  • Design for disassembly and modularity; standardize fasteners; minimize weld-only joins where feasible.
  • Increase recycled content in steel (supplier EPDs) and maximize recyclability of offcuts.
  • Offer refurbishment programs and take-back for select equipment lines.
